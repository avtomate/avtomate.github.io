Affirmation and Stereotype Threat

February 22, 2015

Values I Value

For my assignment in combatting stereotype threat, I will explore the values that have led me to feel the happiest. Some values that allow me to be my happiest include autonomy, efficiency, fairness, and persistence. In general, I try to live up to these values, but I find myself being less efficient than I'd like to be.

The value that's most important to me is autonomy. Being able to complete one's work without necessarily relying on others has always been of great appeal to me. I cannot effectively control the efforts/actions/attitudes of others, but I am always in control of myself, and so I am at my best when I can work on my own.

The last topic someone asked me for advice on was commitment, more specifically, on how to work for hours on end towards some greater end-goal. I've not yet mastered the art myself, but all achievements are just the sum of small habits. My advice is to just make unfaultering commitments to minor habits (e.g. reading for 5 minutes every day), and then progressively building upon them until you're where you'd like to be (e.g. reading for 60 minutes every day.)

I think the greatest realization thus far has been that with enough time, everything is learnable. Accordingly, I shouldn't get discouraged when a person is ostensibly more knowledgable about CS/programming than I am. Even if someone can learn x in one hour when the same learning takes me 3 hours, I can still be more knowledgable by putting in more time and effort. When I'm feeling down, I just need to remind myself to keep a stiff upper-lip and keep moving forward. It sounds cliche, but it's what works.
